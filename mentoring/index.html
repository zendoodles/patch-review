To turn people into participants:

  • announce opportunities to participate, tweets, gratuitous irc factoid use
  • repeat announcements
  • hold local events, repeatedly
  • blog about the benefits
  • get other high profile people to recommend and remind about opportunities to participate in mentoring, slides for presenters at events, talking to active contributors about the benefits of sending people to mentoring

To turn participants into regular participants:

  • make sure people feel welcome
  • match tasks to participants, ensure they are successful the first time
  • keep people engaged even when they struggle
  • help without being overbearing
  • enable folks to continue afterward on their own
  • say hi in irc

Participants into Mentors:

  • Be good mentors (Set a good Example)
  • Answer Questions
  • Ask Them

to turn regular participants into mentors:

  • Ask
  • In a pinch, when there is a real need, ask (busy office hours, someone could not show up and need a replacement, at an in person sprint, ask for volunteers to do a small thing, something they would-be-mentor *is* comfortable with.
  • give would-be mentors a first mentor task they can succeed at. For example, if a new participant is having trouble setting up webserver in linux, ask a would be mentor that you know is comfortable with that. Dont ask a would-be mentor to help someone do a Mac set up, if they dont already know. Dont ask them to help someone with a call back conversion if they have never themselves done one. Save asking them to do something they dont know the answer to for later.
  • help without being overbearing, overhear them in their first mentoring interactions, stay around the same table, watch the conversation in irc.
  • thank them.
  • say one thing they did well. “you were patient when it took them 15 minutes to find their php.ini” “your explanation of interdiffs really helped that new participant”

Turn a first time mentor into a regular mentor

  • continue to be a resource
  • be available for questions
  • participate side by side
  • letting new mentors make mistakes (10 second rule)
  • offering specific suggestions to improve their mentoring

turn a regular mentor into a mentor of mentors

  • ask, ask a regular mentor to show a new mentor how to use mentor tools
  • ask, ask a regular mentor to organize a small event or part of an event where they might have to recruit a couple new mentors
  • publicize, announce, retweet their event or actions
